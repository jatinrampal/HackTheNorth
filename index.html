Futher Information and FAQ

Stats on Insurance Fraud

Auto insurance claims are made for a range of reasons including bodily injury, medical expenses, premium rating errors, and suspicious claims. According to a 2017 Aviva Canada report, approximately $2 billion dollars a year is handed out due to fraudulent claims across Canada. Not only does this cost insurance companies, but it is reported it is costing drivers an extra $100 to $150 per year.

What is blockchain?

Blockchain is a way of adding documents to a database without allowing unwanted outside users to tamper with documents. It allows certain users to access records and can validate information as no one can change the document once it is published.

How does this help prevent auto insurance fraud?

Because blockchain doesn't allow documents to be tampered with after being uploaded, data analytics an be implemented into the system to detect suspicious activity among users. Suspcious ativity is then flagged and reported to the insurance company preventing fruadulent payouts. The software can also calculate insurance premiums and keep track of what each user is paying ensuring valid payments.
